About Mumbai

Mumbai, formerly known as Bombay, is India's most populous city and the financial capital of the country. This vibrant metropolis is a melting pot of cultures, dreams, and opportunities.

From the iconic Gateway of India to the bustling streets of Crawford Market, Mumbai offers an unparalleled urban experience. The city is home to Bollywood, India's largest film industry, and serves as the economic powerhouse of the nation.

With its colonial architecture, modern skyscrapers, street food culture, and the famous local trains that connect the city, Mumbai continues to charm both locals and tourists alike.

Places You Just Can’t Miss

Discover Mumbai's coolest hangout spots and iconic landmarks

Gateway of India

An iconic arch-monument overlooking the Arabian Sea, symbolizing Mumbai’s colonial past and cultural pride

Marine Drive

A picturesque 3.6 km promenade known as the “Queen’s Necklace” for its glittering nighttime view

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus

A UNESCO World Heritage Site blending Victorian Gothic and Indian architecture, and a bustling railway hub

Juhu Beach

A lively beachfront famous for sunsets, street food, and celebrity sightings

Bandra-Worli Sea Link

A modern engineering marvel connecting suburbs with the city, offering stunning views of the sea and skyline

Elephanta Caves

Ancient rock-cut caves with magnificent sculptures dedicated to Lord Shiva, located on Elephanta Island

Siddhivinayak Temple

A revered Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Ganesha, attracting millions of devotees annually

Crawford Market

A vibrant colonial-era market offering fresh produce, spices, and a taste of old Mumbai’s charm

Spicy sprouted bean curry topped with farsan, onions, and coriander, served with buttered pav

Bombay Sandwich

Layered sandwich with chutney, veggies & spice, grilled to perfection

Kanda Bhaji
